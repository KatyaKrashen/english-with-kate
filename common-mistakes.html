1. "I have 25 years old" vs. "I am 25 years old"

In English, we use "am" when talking about age, not "have." So, the correct sentence is "I am 25 years old."

2. "She can sings well" vs. "She can sing well"

After "can," we use the base form of the verb, so the correct sentence is "She can sing well."

3. "I like to go shopping" vs. "I like going shopping"

In English, both are correct, but "I like going shopping" is more commonly used when talking about hobbies or activities.
